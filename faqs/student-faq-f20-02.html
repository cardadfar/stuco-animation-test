I'm still trying to figure that out myself. 8+ years of animation and the only thing I'm a pro at is pro-crastination.

What kind of software would you usually use for digital storyboarding?

Personally? I'd use Photoshop for the reasons that it's very easy to export to other Adobe Applications. Any drawing app that is your favorite is likely to be your favorite storyborading app too since storyboarding is just doodling + note-taking.

I missed the first week, how can I make it up content? And what do I need to prepare?

In general you can start by watching the lecture recordings. Pay attention to the homework slides since I go over expectations and exaples of what you can submit for your weekly quick-edits. If you're still lost, schedule an OH session with me (via email) and we can go over what you missed.

Who are some big name storyboard artists?

This is the same as asking who are some bigh name animation directors. A lot of directors contribute to visual scriping (storyboarding), so their presence isn't just limited to critiquing the animation stages and making executive decision.
